› Introduction

Contextual Design is a user-centered process that that aims to design a product that best meets users’ needs based on gathered, interpreted, and consolidated users’ field data. The gathered field data helps developers better understand users’ requirements, tasks, intents, and desires which will result in developing a software that will match what users truly need.

Contextual Design was developed by Hugh Beyer and Karen Holtzblatt in the late 1980s as a response to industries need for making products more usable. It has been used ever since in many different contexts such as marketing, usability testing, user-centered interface design, etc.
